💰 Total Cash Today ▶

The net physical liquid cash that must match the supervisor's physical vault balance at close of day.

Formula:
(Opening Cash + Opening PD + Cash From Office + Supposed Coll + Recovery + Interest + Forms + Cards + Pay Off + Today's PD)
MINUS
(Used PD + Bank Deposit + Default + Cost of Deals)
Business Example: If an officer comes back with field collections, any cash expenses or bank deposits must be deducted from the gross receipts to find the exact final physical cash volume.
📊 Actual Collection ▶

The true, organic financial yield recovered from the market field during active tracking cycles.

Formula:
Supposed Collection - Default + Recovery + Pay Off + Today's PD - Used PD
Business Example: If a market has a target baseline of ₦100,000 but ₦10,000 defaulted and ₦5,000 extra was brought in via recoveries, the Actual Collection matches ₦95,000.
🔄 Pay Down (PD) System ▶

Understanding Advance Payments: Opening Pay Down vs. Today's Pay Down vs. Used Pay Down.

📌 Opening PD: Surplus funds holding in a client's savings card at start of day.

📌 Today's PD: Brand new extra advance money deposited by the client today.

📌 Used PD: Actively drawing down from their card balance to pay today's daily rate instead of using cash.

🛡️ Recovery & Outstanding Logic ▶

How missed cards handle debt collection and separate isolated transactions from the cell totals.

The Core Rule: When a client defaults, their debt moves to Outstanding. When they pay back extra later, it is flagged as a Recovery.

Real Example: Daily rate = ₦3,600. Client pays ₦4,100 total. The ledger grid saves ₦4,100 to update the bank matrix, but isolates the ₦500 to stamp the sheet comment cleanly as "Recovery of ₦500".
💰 Pay Off Matrix ▶

The final lump-sum liquidation settlement processed to completely close out an active loan tracking file.

Impact Profile:
Increases Actual Collection Total & Physical Cash Balance
System Rule: Reduces Remaining Balance instantly to ₦0.
Business Example: If a client owes a remaining principal balance of ₦15,000 across their remaining 25-day cycle but decides to pay it all down immediately to close the account, this entire bulk amount is recorded as a Pay Off, terminating the tracking loop.
📅 Next Day Collection ▶

Pre-calculated installment projection metric used to prepare field officers for the upcoming morning route run.

Baseline Equation:
Sum of (Active Standard Daily Repayments) for all open non-settled accounts assigned to a specific market track.
Business Example: This metric gives the operations manager an exact target pipeline figure. If a market lane contains 10 active customers who each pay a flat ₦3,600 daily rate, the Next Day Collection baseline target will automatically flag as ₦36,000 for the morning ledger preparation.
